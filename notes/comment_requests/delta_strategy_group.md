## Send-ready

**Subject:** Request for comment — Delta Strategy Group's lobbying of the CFTC

```
Dear Mr. Newsome and Mr. Parsons,

I'm a reporter working on a story about lobbying of the Commodity Futures
Trading Commission by former CFTC officials. Our review of public Lobbying
Disclosure Act filings indicates that:

- Mr. Newsome served as Chairman of the CFTC from 2001 to 2004;
- Mr. Parsons served in senior CFTC roles until approximately 2005;
- Both are now registered lobbyists at Delta Strategy Group. Senate LDA
  filings show 149 of Mr. Parsons' 166 filings (about 90%) and 95 of Mr.
  Newsome's 99 filings (about 96%) target the CFTC.

Because the CFTC is a financial regulator, the public-interest question here
is not federal grants but regulatory access: a former CFTC Chairman and a
former senior CFTC official, at one firm, almost exclusively engaging back
with the agency they once helped lead and regulate. Before publishing, I want
to give you the chance to respond. Specifically:

1. Is our description of your former CFTC roles and current lobbying accurate?
2. Does Delta Strategy Group have a firm-level process to screen its
   principals' CFTC work against the permanent restriction in 18 USC §207(a)(1)
   on matters either of you personally and substantially handled in government?
   Mr. Newsome's tenure as Chairman in particular implicates a broad range of
   specific-party matters from that period.
3. How would you characterize the relationship between your prior CFTC service
   and Delta Strategy Group's current CFTC practice — for example, in terms
   of the rulemakings, enforcement matters, or registration questions on
   which clients seek your representation?
4. Anything else you'd like on the record?

I'd be grateful for a reply by [DEADLINE — 5 business days from send]. I'm
happy to take a written statement or talk by phone.

Thank you,
[Reporter signature]
```
