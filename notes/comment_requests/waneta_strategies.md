```
Dear Mr. Sherman,

I'm a reporter working on a story about lobbying of the Federal Communications
Commission by former FCC officials. Our review of public Lobbying Disclosure
Act filings indicates that:

- You served as Chief of the FCC Wireless Telecommunications Bureau until
  approximately 2015–2016 (please pin the departure date to the month if you
  can);
- You are now a registered lobbyist at Waneta Strategies, where Senate LDA
  filings show 45 of your 45 filings (100%) target the FCC, on behalf of
  wireless and telecom clients including CTIA – The Wireless Association,
  Crown Castle, and Lynk Global.

Because the FCC is a communications regulator, the public-interest question
is regulatory access: a former FCC Wireless Bureau Chief, every one of whose
disclosed lobbying filings targets the FCC, representing major wireless
industry clients before the agency he once led the wireless bureau of.
Before publishing, I want to give you the chance to respond. Specifically:

1. Is our description of your former FCC role and current lobbying accurate?
   Specifically the departure date — we currently have "approximately 2015 or
   2016."
2. Does Waneta Strategies have any process to screen your FCC work against
   the permanent restriction in 18 USC §207(a)(1) on matters you personally
   and substantially handled in government? The Wireless Telecommunications
   Bureau directly oversees spectrum policy, wireless licensing, and tower
   siting — areas central to CTIA's, Crown Castle's, and Lynk's federal
   interests. Did you personally and substantially work on any matter at the
   Bureau that is now the subject of your representation of any of these
   clients?
3. How would you characterize the relationship between your prior FCC service
   and Waneta Strategies' current FCC practice?
4. Anything else you'd like on the record?

I'd be grateful for a reply by [DEADLINE — 5 business days from send]. I'm
happy to take a written statement or talk by phone.

Thank you,
[Reporter signature]
```
