```
Dear Ms. Martin,

I'm a reporter working on a story about lobbying of the Department of the
Interior by former Interior officials. Our review of public Lobbying
Disclosure Act filings indicates that:

- You served in a senior tribal-affairs role at the Department of the
  Interior, including as Acting Assistant Secretary for Indian Affairs,
  until approximately 2005 (please correct or refine);
- You are now a registered lobbyist at Spirit Rock Consulting, where Senate
  LDA filings show 180 of your 264 filings (about 68%) target the Department
  of the Interior, on tribal-affairs matters for tribal-government and
  related clients.

Unlike some other cases in our review, we have not traced a clean federal
money trail to your clients — tribal funding flows through varied programs
and entities, and a recipient-name-verified USAspending.gov pull on these
clients did not produce a confident aggregate. We are framing this case as
one of regulatory access and tribal-policy advocacy, not as a federal-dollars
story. Before publishing, I want to give you the chance to respond.
Specifically:

1. Is our description of your former Interior role and current lobbying
   accurate?
2. Does Spirit Rock Consulting have any process to screen your Interior work
   against the permanent restriction in 18 USC §207(a)(1) on matters you
   personally and substantially handled in government? Tribal-affairs work
   often involves long-running specific-party matters (consent decrees,
   compacts, land-into-trust applications); did you personally and
   substantially work on any such matter that is now the subject of your
   representation?
3. How would you characterize the relationship between your prior Interior
   service and Spirit Rock Consulting's current Interior practice?
4. Which USDA, Interior, BIA, or other federal programs do your tribal
   clients primarily pursue funding or rulemaking through? We want to
   characterize the federal interests at stake accurately rather than
   relying solely on filing-issue codes.
5. Anything else you'd like on the record?

I'd be grateful for a reply by [DEADLINE — 5 business days from send]. I'm
happy to take a written statement or talk by phone.

Thank you,
[Reporter signature]
```
